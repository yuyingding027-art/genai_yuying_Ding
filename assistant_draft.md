The governance of artificial intelligence at the international level is one of the defining challenges of our time. As AI systems become embedded in critical infrastructure, national security decisions, and economic systems, the absence of shared norms and frameworks creates risks that no single country can address alone. Diana is particularly interested in how multilateral institutions, civil society, and governments can collaborate to develop responsible AI governance that is both technically informed and politically viable.
